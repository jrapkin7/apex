YOUR FULL PROGRAM · WEEK 1

Get strong & lean — the healthy way

You're 15 and growing, so we don't cut calories. We upgrade what you eat, train with great form, and move more. That's how you lean out and get stronger at the same time.

Parent + pediatrician first: do this program with your parents, and get your pediatrician's OK before starting. No dieting to lose weight at your age, no creatine, no pre-workout, and no peptides — real food and good training do the work.

Form-first rule: leave 2 reps in the tank, control every rep, and never max out. Master the movement before you add weight — ask a parent or coach to check your form. Log every set on the Today tab.

Activity & cardio

  • Move every day — aim 8–10k steps. Sports totally count.
  • Play the sports you like — basketball, biking, swimming. Fun + consistent beats forced.

Nutrition — upgrade your plate

  • More real protein — eggs, chicken, turkey, Greek yogurt, milk at every meal.
  • Add a vegetable or fruit to every meal — color on the plate.
  • Swap sugary drinks for water or milk.
  • Mix it up — less repeating the same smoothie + quesadilla, more variety.

Documentation — daily

  • Day-1 photos + measurements today.
  • Weigh in 1–2×/week (Today tab).
  • Quick note on how training and food went.
  • Weekly: photos + a 2-min recap with your dad.

Supplements

  • None needed — food does the work. No creatine, no pre-workout, no peptides. A multivitamin or vitamin D only if your pediatrician recommends it.

The one rule: protein + a vegetable or fruit at every meal, and swap sugary drinks for water or milk. Do that and the rest takes care of itself.

Grocery staples to keep around

  • Protein: eggs, chicken, ground turkey, Greek yogurt, milk, cheese, jerky, whey.
  • Carbs: whole-grain bread/wraps, rice, oats, potatoes, fruit.
  • Veg & fats: any veggies you like, peanut butter, nuts, avocado, olive oil.
  • Grab-and-go: pre-portion yogurt, cheese sticks, fruit, and turkey so the easy choice is the good one.

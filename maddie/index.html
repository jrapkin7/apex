YOUR FULL PROGRAM · WEEK 1

Rebuild strength — gentle & sustainable

Built for where you are right now: strength, movement, and nourishing food to help you feel like yourself again and bring inflammation down. Nothing intense — a steady, kind pace, especially with a baby hopefully on the horizon.

Your doctor signs off first. Based on what you shared, none of this loads in until your doctor gives the OK — and they may adjust the calories or movements to fit exactly where your body is right now.

Daily targets

1,750cal

A gentle ~230 cal/day deficit — steady fat loss without stressing your body. Protein holds your muscle; the fat supports your hormones. Not a crash diet.

Training — home basics, 3–4 days

All bodyweight — no equipment needed. Everything here you can do in your living room. Once a move feels easy, add a resistance band, light dumbbells, or even a couple of water bottles for a little more — totally optional.

Core is deliberately gentle: dead bugs, bird-dogs, side planks, Pallof presses — controlled, low-strain work. No crunches, sit-ups, or breath-holding. If anything feels off, stop and mention it to your coach or doctor.
Pace: leave 3–4 reps in the tank, breathe through every rep, and never grind. This is about consistency and feeling good, not pushing to failure. Rotate A → B → C across your 3–4 days.

Walking & recovery

  • Daily walk — 20–40 min, any pace (the single best thing for inflammation, mood, and gentle fat loss right now).
  • Sleep + stress — protect your 7–8 hours; gentle yoga or mobility on off days.

Nutrition — nourish & reduce inflammation

  • Protein every meal — eggs, chicken, salmon, Greek yogurt (~30g each).
  • Anti-inflammatory foods — leafy greens, berries, olive oil, fatty fish, nuts, turmeric, colorful veg.
  • Enough healthy fat — avocado, olive oil, salmon, walnuts, flax (your hormones need it).
  • You cook most meals — lean into that; whole foods over packaged.

Documentation — weekly

  • Photos + measurements weekly, not daily.
  • Journal how you feel — energy, mood, bloating/inflammation, sleep.
  • Weigh in weekly if it helps; skip it if it doesn't serve you.

Supplements

  • Ask your doctor about a prenatal + omega-3 if you're trying to conceive. Otherwise vitamin D, magnesium, and enough protein. Clear everything with your doctor first.
